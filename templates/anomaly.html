Status Codes Distribution
A status codes graph displays the distribution of HTTP status codes versus their frequency in a dataset or system. HTTP status codes, ranging from 100 to 599, signify the outcome of client-server interactions, such as success (2xx), redirection (3xx), client errors (4xx), or server errors (5xx). Each status code is represented by a bar or point on the graph, with the height or position indicating its frequency. This visualization allows users to quickly identify common status codes and potential issues, aiding in the monitoring and optimization of web services.
Traffic Label Distribution
Traffic Label Distribution refers to the distribution or breakdown of different types of network traffic within a network. It provides insights into the composition and characteristics of the traffic traversing a network infrastructure. These labels can include various categories such as web browsing, file transfers, video streaming, VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol), gaming, and more. Analyzing the traffic label distribution helps network administrators understand the nature of the network usage, identify potential bottlenecks or security threats, optimize network resources, and tailor network policies or configurations to better accommodate the predominant types of traffic. Overall, it enables efficient management and monitoring of network traffic to ensure optimal performance and security.
